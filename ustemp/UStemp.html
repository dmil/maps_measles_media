Prevalence of Measles in the U.S. Over Time

Evaluated on a State-by-State Basis from 19XX-20XX

Measles was once thought to be eradicated in the United States. After decades through America’s early history, national vigilance around vaccination supported the country’s herd immunity to such a degree that the disease seemed like part of the country’s past rather than present. Throughout recent decades, however, measles has become increasingly difficult to control, with outbreaks becoming more commonplace amidst faltering vaccination rates.
